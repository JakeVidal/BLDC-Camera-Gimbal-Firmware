Meet The Team

Alexander Cote

Alex was responsible for the hardware design of the system. This includes designing the power system, the system schematic, selecting components, and routing the PCB design.

Clinton Bestmann

Clinton was responsible for developing a serial communication protocol for interfacing the gimbal control system with both the outside world and with the object tracking system.

Jake Vidal

Jake was responsible for the development of motor control firmware and development of the overall firmware structure. Jake was also responsible for reviewing hardware designs produced by Alex and assisting Noah with global system control.

Noah Rondeau

Noah was responsible for the global system architecture of the firmware. He was also responsible for determining the pinout of the microcontroller for use in the hardware design and coordinating team interactions with AERO and our supervisor.

Tyler Fisher

Tyler was responsible for the object tracking subsystem. This includes designing and implementing an algorithm for analyzing images, tracking object motion, and determining angular deltas to send to the gimbal as commands.
